# Simple BAA Template (Management Company ↔ Subsidiary Company)

> **Important:** This is a lightweight starting template, not legal advice. Have counsel review.

## Parties
This Business Associate Agreement (“BAA”) is entered into by and between:

- **Covered Entity:** __________________________ (“CE”)
- **Business Associate:** _______________________ (“BA”)

Effective Date: __________________

## Purpose
BA provides administrative and scheduling services to CE that may require access to Protected Health Information (“PHI”). This BAA permits BA to create, receive, maintain, or transmit PHI on behalf of CE, consistent with HIPAA.

## Permitted Uses and Disclosures
BA may use/disclose PHI only to:
- Perform scheduling, task assignment, and related administrative functions for CE;
- Manage BA’s internal operations as permitted by HIPAA (including quality assurance and audit logging), provided PHI is protected;
- Comply with legal requirements.

BA will not use/disclose PHI in a manner that would violate HIPAA if done by CE.

## Safeguards
BA will:
- Implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to protect PHI;
- Limit PHI to the minimum necessary for scheduling/task workflows;
- Ensure workforce members with access are trained and authorized.

## Subcontractors
BA will ensure any subcontractors who access PHI agree in writing to restrictions and safeguards no less stringent than this BAA.

## Breach Notification
BA will notify CE without unreasonable delay, and no later than ____ days, after discovery of any breach of unsecured PHI, and will provide relevant details as available.

## Access, Amendment, Accounting
To the extent applicable, BA will assist CE in responding to:
- Individual requests for access or amendment of PHI;
- Requests for an accounting of disclosures.

## Term and Termination
- This BAA remains in effect until terminated.
- Either party may terminate for material breach after reasonable notice and opportunity to cure.
- Upon termination, BA will return or destroy PHI if feasible; if not feasible, BA will continue to protect PHI and limit use/disclosure.

## Miscellaneous
- This BAA is governed by HIPAA and applicable state law.
- If any provision conflicts with HIPAA, HIPAA controls.
